The happy path is useful for finding the shape of a product. It is rarely enough to make the product trustworthy.

## Start where confidence drops

Look for moments where the interface asks someone to guess: an unfamiliar status, a disabled action without a reason, or an error that names a problem but offers no next step.

The last ten percent is not polish. It is the work of making the system understandable when the situation is unusual, interrupted, or high stakes.

## Design the recovery path

Good recovery states answer three questions: what happened, what remains safe, and what the person can do next. That clarity is part of the core experience, not a message added after the feature is finished.
